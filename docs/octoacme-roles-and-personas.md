# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
Responsible for shaping and validating the user experience across the product lifecycle, including research, design, usability testing, and collaboration with development and product teams.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and synthesize findings into actionable insights
- Deliver user flows, wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Validate usability through testing and feedback collection
- Collaborate closely with Product Managers to translate requirements into user-centric solutions
- Support Developers and QA in implementing and refining UI components

### Goals
- Ensure the product is intuitive, accessible, and delightful for end-users
- Raise usability issues and advocate for improvements early
- Align design outcomes with business and product objectives

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings
- Regular syncs with Product Managers, Developers, and QA
- Usability testing reports and design documentation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Bridges business goals and technical implementation by capturing requirements, analyzing processes, and ensuring delivered solutions meet business needs.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document requirements from stakeholders and users
- Analyze business processes and suggest optimizations
- Translate high-level requirements into detailed user stories or specifications
- Assist in testing and acceptance validation
- Align all parties on scope and expected business outcomes

### Goals
- Ensure that technical solutions solve the true business problem
- Reduce ambiguity throughout planning and execution
- Serve as a "source of truth" for requirement clarifications

### Typical Communication
- Workshops and interviews with stakeholders
- Requirement documents, diagrams, and user stories
- Ongoing support to Developers and Product Managers

---

## Site Reliability Engineer (SRE)

### Role Summary
Ensures reliability, scalability, and performance of the product in production. Guides best practices for monitoring, incident management, automation, and operational readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain monitoring and alerting systems
- Collaborate on deployment and rollout strategies
- Set up incident response procedures and participate in incident management
- Guide Developers and QA on reliability and scalability standards
- Review infrastructure changes and ensure compliance with SLAs

### Goals
- Improve uptime and reduce incidents or downtime
- Accelerate incident resolution and postmortem learning cycles
- Increase automation for operations

### Typical Communication
- Incident postmortems and reliability reports
- Delivery planning for high-risk releases
- Consultations with Product Managers, Developers, and QA

---

## Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Facilitates team adoption of agile best practices, removes process impediments, and fosters continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Coach the team in agile methodologies and ceremonies
- Facilitate retrospectives, helping teams reflect and adapt
- Mentor Scrum Masters, PMs, and Product Managers on process health
- Identify and help resolve systemic process issues
- Track and visualize process improvement metrics

### Goals
- Foster self-organizing, high-performing teams
- Ensure continuous, measurable process improvement
- Support organizational agility and responsiveness

### Typical Communication
- Agile ceremonies (retrospectives, standups, planning)
- 1:1s with team members and leads
- Process documentation and training materials
